Before Your Speech

1. Know your material. Don't memorize word-for-word. Understand the concepts.
2. Practice out loud. Not in your head. Actually speak the words.
3. Record yourself. Watch it back. Fix awkward habits.
4. Visit the venue. Familiarize yourself with the space.
5. Prepare for questions. Anticipate what people might ask.

Overcome Nervousness

6. Reframe anxiety as excitement. Same physical feeling, different label.
7. Breathe deeply. 4 seconds in, 4 seconds out. Calms your nervous system.
8. Power pose. 2 minutes of expansive posture before speaking.
9. Focus on the message, not yourself. You're there to help the audience.
10. Remember: They want you to succeed. Audiences root for speakers.

During Your Speech

11. Start strong. Hook them in the first 30 seconds.
12. Make eye contact. Pick 3-4 friendly faces in different sections.
13. Use pauses. Silence is powerful. Don't fill it with "um" or "uh".
14. Move with purpose. Don't pace. Plant your feet, then move intentionally.
15. Vary your voice. Speed, volume, pitch. Monotone kills attention.
16. Tell stories. People remember stories, not statistics.
17. End with a call to action. What should they do next?

Structure That Works

The Rule of Three:
1. Tell them what you'll tell them
2. Tell them
3. Tell them what you told them
"The best speakers know that speaking is not about impressing - it's about serving the audience."
